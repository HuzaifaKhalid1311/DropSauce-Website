What it is

A practical reader with a little expressive bite.

For everyday reading

DropSauce keeps the main loop quick: find a title through sources you add, save it, read it, and pick up from the same spot later. The UI is built around dense manga libraries, not a storefront.

For people who tinker

Extension management, source settings, backups, import tools, tracking, and reader controls are available without hiding everything behind a cloud account.

For open-source use

The app is published under GPLv3, with release APKs on GitHub and code open for inspection, fixes, and contributions.

App tour

The important screens, minus the brochure voice.

Favorites, categories, progress

Make a library that remembers where you are.

Favorites, read-later lists, history, bookmarks, reading progress, and stats all sit close to the reading flow, so the app feels useful after the first chapter.

Reader controls

Reader chrome that gets out of the page.

The reader supports comfortable navigation, gestures, progress controls, cleaner haptics, and modes suited to normal pages or long webtoon-style chapters.

Extensions

Add sources yourself and keep the reader neutral.

DropSauce ships as a tool, not a catalog. You choose source and extension repositories, manage them in-app, and stay responsible for the content you access.

Settings and sync

Sync, backup, lock, import, and tune without a service account.

Google Drive sync is optional. Local backups, PDF import, reader settings, storage controls, app lock, widgets, and update checks are available from the same Android-first settings surface.

Important by design

DropSauce is not a manga provider.

The app itself does not host, distribute, or bundle manga. It is a reader and library manager for sources you choose to add. That distinction is part of the product, not a footnote.

Data handling

Offline by default. Sync only when you choose it.

DropSauce has no backend server. Without sync, app data remains on your device. When Google Drive sync is enabled, the app reads and writes its own synchronization file in your Drive account so your library can move between devices.

storage

Local data includes favorites, categories, history, bookmarks, tracking, stats, and settings.

visibility_off

The app cannot read or modify arbitrary files in your Google Drive.

delete

You can sign out, delete synced data, revoke access, or uninstall to remove local data.
